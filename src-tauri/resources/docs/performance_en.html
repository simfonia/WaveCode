Performance: Triggering Sounds

WaveCode performance blocks bridge your logic to the high-performance Rust-based fundsp engine. We use a professional Note On/Off architecture, fully compatible with external MIDI controllers and internal sequencers.

High-Performance Polyphony: The WaveCode engine supports 8-voice polyphony for each instrument definition. This allows complex chords and overlapping notes to play smoothly without cutting each other off.

1. Synth Start (Note On)

Instantiates a sound node in the fundsp signal chain and triggers the Attack phase of the ADSR envelope. The sound will sustain its energy according to your settings until a corresponding "Stop" command is received.

2. Synth Stop (Note Off)

Signals the active voice to enter the Release phase. The sound will naturally fade out based on your Release time (R), ensuring a smooth musical transition instead of an abrupt click.

3. Status Detection & Exit

  • Is any melody playing?: Checks the status of the internal sequence buffer. Returns true if any notes are still queued or being processed.
  • Exit Program: Gracefully shuts down the WaveCode Engine and closes the stage window. It is recommended to use this after detecting that all melodies have finished playing.

4. Real-time Node Control

Because WaveCode is powered by fundsp, parameter changes (like frequency or volume) are processed at the sample level, allowing for ultra-smooth sweeps and glitch-free performance.
